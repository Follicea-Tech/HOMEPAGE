Collaboration Agreement with Kyoto University Research Institute

📅 March 2025 🤝 Partnership Announcement 📍 Kyoto, Japan

Follicea Technologies is pleased to announce the signing of a formal research collaboration agreement with a leading reproductive biology research institute at Kyoto University. This partnership establishes a foundation for shared scientific inquiry and accelerates our path toward identifying and validating novel molecular targets for infertility therapeutics.

About the Partnership

The collaboration brings together Follicea's drug discovery capabilities with the institute's deep expertise in ovarian biology, folliculogenesis, and reproductive endocrinology. The agreement provides Follicea with access to validated model systems, proprietary datasets, and the insights of researchers who have spent decades studying the molecular mechanisms of female reproduction.

Kyoto University has long been recognized as one of Japan's premier institutions for reproductive medicine research. Its scientists have contributed foundational work on the cellular and molecular events governing oocyte development and ovarian function — precisely the biological domain at the core of Follicea's therapeutic focus.

Scope of collaboration: Joint target validation studies, access to primary follicular cell culture systems, co-analysis of genomic datasets, and advisory input on candidate prioritization throughout the lead identification phase.

Why Academic Collaboration Matters at This Stage

Early-stage drug discovery is a discipline where the gap between published literature and experimental reality is wide. High-quality academic collaborators close that gap by providing hands-on biological expertise, access to well-characterized model systems, and critical perspective on which findings are robust and which are artifacts of the assay.

For a company focused on a biologically complex area like folliculogenesis, working alongside scientists who have built careers studying the precise mechanisms we are targeting is not a luxury — it is a scientific necessity. This agreement reflects our conviction that the best drug discovery happens at the boundary between industry-focused execution and academic rigor.

Translating deep academic knowledge of reproductive biology into therapeutic hypotheses requires both scientific insight and rigorous drug discovery methodology. This collaboration is designed to bring both to bear from the very beginning.

— Scientific Team, Follicea Technologies

Structure of the Agreement

The collaboration is structured as a multi-year research partnership with defined milestones tied to Follicea's drug discovery roadmap. Key elements include:

  • Shared access to primary granulosa cell and oocyte model systems
  • Joint analysis of transcriptomic and proteomic datasets from follicular tissue
  • Quarterly scientific advisory meetings with senior institute researchers
  • Co-development of functional assays for candidate target validation
  • Framework for potential co-authorship on resulting publications

Next Steps and Anticipated Outcomes

The collaboration is already underway, with joint work integrated into Follicea's ongoing lead target identification program. We expect the partnership to directly contribute to our planned lead target nomination in Q2 2025, providing biological validation for the computational candidates identified through our in-house screening.

Looking further ahead, the academic relationship established through this agreement is expected to continue into the hit generation and lead optimization phases of our pipeline, providing ongoing biological insight as candidate molecules become increasingly specific and chemically defined.

Looking ahead: Additional research collaboration agreements with institutions across Japan and internationally are under discussion. We will announce further partnerships as agreements are finalized.

About Follicea Technologies

Follicea Technologies, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotech startup based in Kyoto, Japan, focused on the discovery and development of small molecule therapeutics for infertility. For more information, visit our website or contact us at info@folliceatech.com.
