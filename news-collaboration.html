Collaboration Agreement with Kyoto University Research Institute

📅 February 2025 🤝 Partnership 📍 Kyoto, Japan

Follicea Technologies is pleased to announce the signing of a formal research collaboration agreement with a research institute at Kyoto University. This partnership provides Follicea with access to world-class reproductive biology expertise and state-of-the-art experimental infrastructure, establishing a foundational scientific relationship that will accelerate our early-stage drug discovery program.

Why This Partnership Matters

For a biotech startup at the drug discovery stage, academic collaboration is not merely supplementary — it is often the difference between scientifically robust programs and ones that fail for avoidable reasons. Kyoto University's research strengths in reproductive biology, molecular endocrinology, and in vivo model systems are directly aligned with the scientific challenges at the core of our lead program.

This agreement allows our internal discovery team to work alongside researchers who have spent years building expertise and infrastructure in precisely the areas we need. It is a relationship built on scientific complementarity, mutual respect, and a shared conviction that mechanistic approaches to infertility are the right path forward.

Scope of collaboration: The agreement covers joint research activities in follicular biology, access to validated in vivo reproductive models, and shared scientific review of Follicea's lead target portfolio. The initial term runs through Q4 2025, with an option to extend.

What the Collaboration Covers

The collaboration is structured around four core areas of joint activity, each addressing a specific scientific need in Follicea's current discovery program:

Target Biology Validation

Independent biological validation of Follicea's lead targets using the institute's established cell and tissue model systems for follicular development.

In Vivo Model Access

Access to reproductive biology in vivo models for phenotypic characterization of target perturbation, supporting early efficacy and safety assessment.

Genomic and Proteomic Data

Shared access to curated transcriptomic and proteomic datasets from human and model follicular tissue, enriching Follicea's computational biology pipeline.

Scientific Advisory Input

Structured scientific advisory sessions with senior reproductive biology researchers to review and challenge Follicea's target rationale and discovery strategy.

Kyoto University and the Life Sciences Ecosystem

Kyoto University is consistently ranked among Japan's top research institutions and has a longstanding reputation for excellence in basic biological sciences. Its medical and life science faculties include internationally recognized researchers in reproductive medicine, developmental biology, and molecular endocrinology.

One of the reasons Follicea chose to headquarter in Kyoto was precisely the opportunity this institutional proximity represents. Building close, ongoing relationships with university researchers — rather than arm's-length contract relationships — is part of how we intend to do science differently: with depth, with intellectual honesty, and with the kind of rigor that only comes from having expert collaborators who will push back when they see flaws in our thinking.

Being embedded in Kyoto's academic environment isn't just geographically convenient — it's scientifically essential. We want our work to be tested and challenged by people who know this biology better than anyone.

— Founding Team, Follicea Technologies

Intellectual Property and Governance

The collaboration agreement includes clearly defined IP governance provisions that protect both parties' interests while enabling free and open scientific exchange within the collaboration. Background IP contributed by each party is retained by that party. Jointly developed foreground IP arising from collaboration activities will be co-owned, with commercialization rights structured to facilitate Follicea's development and licensing activities.

Both institutions are committed to the prompt publication of scientific findings, subject to standard IP protection review periods. We believe transparency and rigorous peer review are essential to advancing the field, and this commitment is reflected in the agreement's publication provisions.

Looking Ahead

The collaboration formally commenced in February 2025, with initial joint activities focused on target validation experiments. Key milestones anticipated under the agreement include:

  • First joint target validation experiments (Q1 2025)
  • In vivo model establishment for lead target series (Q2 2025)
  • Joint scientific review of target portfolio ahead of lead nomination (Q2 2025)
  • Extended collaboration discussions based on Q2 progress review (Q3 2025)

We are grateful to our collaborators at Kyoto University for their enthusiasm for this work and their willingness to engage deeply with the science from the earliest stages of our program. This partnership is a reflection of the kind of rigorous, collaborative approach we intend to carry through every stage of our development.

About Follicea Technologies

Follicea Technologies, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotech startup based in Kyoto, Japan, focused on the discovery and development of small molecule therapeutics for infertility. The company is currently in the drug discovery stage, with a lead program targeting a novel pathway in follicular development. For more information, visit our website or contact us at info@folliceatech.com.
