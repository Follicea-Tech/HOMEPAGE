Our Technology Platform

A detailed look at the science driving Follicea Technologies — from in silico virtual screening to automated synthesis, robotic high-throughput assays, and organoid validation.

The Problem

Infertility Remains Largely Underserved

Infertility affects approximately 1 in 6 people of reproductive age worldwide, yet the therapeutic landscape has seen limited pharmacological innovation in recent decades. Most treatments focus on symptomatic management — hormonal stimulation — rather than addressing the underlying molecular causes. Follicea Technologies was founded on the premise that precision, mechanism-targeted drugs can do better.

Our Approach

Computation-Driven, Experimentally Validated

Our platform closes the loop between in silico prediction and experimental reality — rapidly cycling through virtual screening, automated synthesis, robotic assays, and organoid validation to identify and optimize drug candidates at unprecedented speed. What once required hundreds of researchers and years of work, we accomplish with a small team through deep integration of computation and automation.

1

In Silico Screening

We use molecular docking and virtual high-throughput screening (vHTS) to evaluate hundreds of millions of compounds against validated target structures. Physics-based models combined with machine learning predict binding affinity, selectivity, and early ADMET properties — dramatically narrowing the candidate pool before a single synthesis is performed.

2

Automated Peptide Synthesis

Computationally selected sequences enter our fully automated synthesis platform, capable of producing up to 18,000 peptide variants per month. Modular synthesis robots with inline MS/HPLC quality verification translate virtual candidates into physical samples within hours — bypassing traditional synthesis bottlenecks entirely.

3

Robotic High-Throughput Screening

Synthesized compounds feed directly into our automated assay platform. Liquid handling robots, 384/1,536 well plate formats, and barcode-tracked compound storage operate 24/7, generating hundreds of thousands of data points per day. AI analysis of results feeds immediately back into the next in silico cycle.

4

Organoid Validation

Prioritized hits are validated in patient-derived organoid models — three-dimensional tissue constructs that recapitulate disease biology with clinical-grade fidelity. HTS-compatible organoid systems (microwell arrays, PEG substrates, microfluidic encapsulation) enable parallel efficacy and organ-specific safety evaluation before committing to in vivo studies.

Platform

Core Technology Pillars

Four tightly integrated capabilities form our drug discovery engine — each pillar amplifying the others to accelerate the path from molecular hypothesis to clinical candidate.

In Silico Drug Design

Our proprietary computational platform uses molecular docking and virtual high-throughput screening (vHTS) to evaluate hundreds of millions of compounds against target protein structures. Physics-based models and deep learning are combined to predict binding affinity, selectivity, and ADMET properties before any synthesis is performed.

  • Molecular docking & virtual HTS (vHTS)
  • AI/ML-based affinity & selectivity prediction
  • Integrated ADMET & pharmacokinetics modeling
  • Fragment-based and structure-guided design

High-Throughput Peptide Synthesis

Computationally selected sequences enter our fully automated synthesis platform. Modular synthesis robots with inline MS/HPLC quality verification produce up to 18,000 peptide variants per month — translating virtual candidates into physical samples within hours, not weeks.

  • Up to 18,000 peptide variants per month
  • 600+ 15-residue sequences synthesized per 24 hours
  • Cyclic, modified & stapled peptides supported
  • Inline mass spec & HPLC quality verification

Automated Robotic Screening

Synthesized compounds feed directly into our automated assay platform. Liquid handling robots, 384/1,536 well microplate formats, and barcode-tracked compound storage operate 24/7, generating hundreds of thousands of reproducible data points per day. AI analysis feeds results back into the next computation cycle.

  • 384 & 1,536 well plate HTS formats
  • Liquid handling robots with 3D barcode tracking
  • 24/7 unattended operation
  • AI-driven data analysis & iterative feedback

Organoid Assays

Prioritized hits are validated in patient-derived organoid models — three-dimensional tissue constructs that recapitulate disease physiology with high clinical fidelity. HTS-compatible organoid systems enable drug response assessment at scale, with demonstrated 88–100% clinical prediction accuracy in peer-reviewed studies.

  • Patient-derived 3D tissue models (multi-organ panel)
  • 88–100% clinical response prediction accuracy
  • HTS-compatible microwell & PEG substrate systems
  • Brain organoids via microfluidic encapsulation

Research Focus

Scientific Focus Areas

Our target selection is guided by unmet clinical need and biological tractability across these key areas of reproductive medicine.

Ovarian Biology

Folliculogenesis & Ovulation

Understanding and restoring the regulated process of follicle recruitment, development, and ovulation — disrupted in anovulatory conditions such as PCOS and hypothalamic amenorrhea.

Oocyte Quality

Oocyte Maturation & Competence

Addressing molecular defects in oocyte meiotic maturation and mitochondrial function that contribute to poor embryo quality — a major unmet need in older reproductive-age patients.

Endocrinology

Hormonal Axis Modulation

Small molecule modulators of the HPG axis that can fine-tune FSH and LH signaling with greater specificity and fewer side effects than current injectable gonadotropin therapies.

Male Infertility

Spermatogenesis Support

Although our lead program targets female infertility, we are actively evaluating male-factor indications where targeted small molecule intervention may restore spermatogenesis.

Implantation

Endometrial Receptivity

Investigating molecular determinants of endometrial window-of-implantation timing and receptivity to support embryo implantation success — relevant for both natural and ART cycles.

Precision Medicine

Patient Stratification

Developing biomarker strategies to identify patient sub-populations most likely to respond to our therapies, maximizing clinical benefit and supporting regulatory approval.
